Structurely · Pricing Model Flow

Token-based subscription with rolling billing & monthly usage reconciliation

Step 1 · Sales
Client Provides Monthly Budget / Token Limit
Sales must consult to translate budget → token needs. Clients may have a dollar figure but are unlikely to know token volume. Sales owns this estimation. Both the subscription fee and token usage budget are confirmed and collected together at this stage — before signing.
Step 2 · Signup & Payment
Client Pays Implementation Fee + Subscription Fee; Token Budget Set
Both subscription and token usage run on the same rolling cycle starting from signup date. Token budget was confirmed in Step 1 — no separate collection needed here. Subscription renews monthly on the cycle date; usage is invoiced on the 15th of the month after the cycle closes.

Example: A client on an April 20–May 20 cycle is billed for their subscription fee on April 20, and their usage fee on June 15.
Step 3 · Daily Manual Monitoring
Token Usage Tracked Manually Every Day
There is no automated tracking at this stage. CS/sales must manually log daily record counts of token usage to monitor consumption against the client's set limit. Day-by-day tracking is required to support daily overage billing.
~90% of token limit reached
Step 4 · Trigger
Sales Reaches Out to Client
Proactive touchpoint — present options, understand client intent for the remainder of the month.
Option A
Maintain Budget
Client Stays at Current Limit
Once 100% is reached, AI conversations stop for the remainder of the billing cycle. No addendum needed.
Option B
Continue Beyond Budget
Client Opts to Go Over Limit
Client continues using beyond their token budget. Overage is billed daily based on utilization for each day they exceed their limit. Manually processed by CS/sales.
Step 5 · Usage Billing · 15th of Month After Cycle Close
Usage Invoice(s) Issued via Stripe
e.g. Cycle: Apr 20 – May 20 → Usage billed June 15
If ≤ 100% of base budget
Single Usage Bill
One Stripe charge for the cycle's usage. Straightforward.
If client went over budget
Daily Overage Charges + Base Bill
Overage was billed daily throughout the cycle. Base usage bill still issued on the 15th at cycle close. Multiple Stripe charges.
⚠ Non-Payment
Feature Flag Activated if Payment Missed
If a client does not pay, a feature flag is triggered to shut down outbound AI conversations. Inbound cannot currently be shut down. Manual follow-up required by CS/sales.
Step 6 · Cycle Repeats
New Billing Cycle Begins — Reset & Review
If client is consistently at or above their token budget, sales must work with them to increase the standard monthly budget. Treat as an expansion opportunity, not just an overage flag.

Note: Clients cannot downgrade their token budget themselves — any budget changes must go through sales/CS.
