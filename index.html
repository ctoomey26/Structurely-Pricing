Structurely · Pricing Model Flow

At signup: implementation fee, subscription, and prepaid token allotment — subscription and allotment share one rolling cycle from signup.

If the allotment runs out before the cycle ends: pause until the next cycle, or agree to daily overage (calculated each day, charged in Stripe the next day). Unpaid overage pauses the account until it is settled.

Step 1 · Sales
Scope the Plan Before Signing
Sales works with the prospect to choose the right subscription tier and a realistic monthly token amount (and budget). Fees are not collected here — that happens at signup in Step 2.
Step 2 · Signup & Payment
Implementation + Subscription + Prepaid Token Allotment
At signup the client pays the implementation fee, subscription, and the agreed prepaid token allotment for the cycle. Subscription renewals and the token allotment reset on the same rolling cycle (anchored to signup). If they exhaust the allotment before the cycle ends, they either pause until the next cycle or agree to daily overage — calculated each day and charged in Stripe the following day. Unpaid overage pauses the account until it is settled.
Step 3 · Usage Monitoring
Watch Consumption Against the Allotment
Monitor token usage so you can see who is approaching their prepaid limit and may need a proactive conversation before the cycle ends.
~90% of prepaid allotment reached
Step 4 · Trigger
Sales Reaches Out to Client
Proactive touchpoint — present options, understand client intent for the remainder of the billing cycle.
Option A
Maintain Budget
Pause Until Next Cycle
Once 100% of the allotment is used, outbound is paused for the rest of the cycle. Inbound stays operational with today's product limits. No further outbound use until the next cycle begins.
Option B
Daily Overage
Client Agrees to Continue Past the Allotment
Overage is calculated per day; each day's overage is charged in Stripe the next day. Miss an overage payment → account pauses until that balance is paid.
⚠ Non-Payment (Option B)
Account Paused Until Overage Is Paid
Unpaid overage amounts pause the account until settled. Outbound is shut off via feature flag; inbound remains operational with today's product limits.
Step 6 · Cycle Repeats
New Billing Cycle Begins — Reset & Review
If a client is consistently at or above their prepaid allotment, sales should work with them to raise the standard subscription + token package — treat it as expansion, not only as overage.

Note: Clients cannot downgrade their token allotment themselves; changes go through sales/CS.

Important · How money moves

At signup the client pays implementation, subscription, and the agreed prepaid token allotment. Subscription renewals and the token allotment reset on the same rolling cycle from signup. If the allotment is exhausted before the cycle ends, they either pause outbound until the next cycle (inbound can stay on with current product limits) or agree to daily overage — we calculate overage each day and charge it in Stripe the following day.
